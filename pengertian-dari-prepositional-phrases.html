In the enchanting forest, a small cottage nestled among the tall trees, providing a peaceful retreat for weary travelers. Inside the cottage, a warm fireplace crackles, creating a cozy atmosphere on chilly evenings.

**Adjective Clause:**

The suspense that built up throughout the story kept me engaged until the very last page. The author, whose previous works I had enjoyed, once again demonstrated their mastery of storytelling.

**Penjelasan:**

**Prepositional Phrases (Frasa Preposisi):**

Prepositional phrases adalah rangkaian kata yang dimulai dengan kata depan (preposition) dan biasanya diikuti oleh kata benda atau kata ganti (noun or pronoun). Frasa preposisi digunakan untuk memberikan informasi tambahan tentang lokasi, waktu, tujuan, atau hubungan lain antara elemen dalam kalimat. Dalam paragraf di atas, frasa preposisi digunakan untuk memberikan informasi lebih lanjut tentang tempat (enchanting forest, among the tall trees), jalur (through the woods), dan lokasi (in the cottage, inside the cottage).

**Adjective Clause (Klausa Sifat):**

Adjective clause adalah sekelompok kata yang berfungsi sebagai klausa dan memberikan informasi lebih lanjut tentang kata benda (noun) dalam kalimat. Klausa sifat sering dimulai dengan kata penghubung (relative pronoun) seperti "that," "who," "which," "whom," atau "whose." Klausa sifat memberikan deskripsi atau informasi tambahan tentang subjek atau objek dalam kalimat. Dalam paragraf di atas, klausa sifat digunakan untuk memberikan informasi tambahan tentang buku (that I borrowed from the library), protagonis (who was a brilliant detective), suspense (that built up throughout the story), dan penulis (whose previous works I had enjoyed).

In English grammar, a prepositional phrase is a group of words made up of a preposition (such as to, with, or across), its object (a noun or pronoun), and any of the object's modifiers (an article and/or an adjective). It is only a portion of a sentence and cannot stand on its own as a complete thought. Prepositional phrases often tell where something happened, when it happened, or specify which one. Because of these functions, they're often essential to understanding a sentence.

Key Takeaways: Prepositional Phrases

Prepositional phrases are groups of words starting with a preposition.

Prepositional phrases often function as modifiers, describing nouns and verbs.

Phrases can't stand alone. A prepositional phrase won't contain the subject of a sentence.

19. Decide whether the prepositional phrases in the sentences are used as adjectives or adverbs​


Decide whether the prepositional phrases in the sentences are used as adjectives or adverbs.

a. The river outside the boundary is dangerous to cross.

(Sungai di sebelah luar perbatasan itu berbahaya untuk disebrangi)

ANSWER: Prepositional phrase as adjective.

'outside the boundary' tells which river is dangerous to cross. 'Di sebelah luar perbatasan' menyatakan sungai mana yang berbahaya untuk disebrangi. Maka, prepositional phrase ini berfungsi sebagai kata sifat (adjective).

b. All the passengers aboard the bullet train were frightened when they heard the explosion.

(Semua penumpang yang naik kereta peluru merasa takut ketika mereka mendengar ledakan)

ANSWER: Prepositional phrase as adjective.

'aboard the bullet train' tells which passengers were frightened when they heard explosion. 'yang naik kereta peluru' menyatakan penumpang takut ketika mereka mendengar ledakan. Maka, prepositional phrase ini berfungsi sebagai kata sifat (adjective).

c. My shopping list needs to be put into my purse, so that I won't forget it.

(Daftar belanjaku perlu dimasukkan ke dalam dompetku, sehingga I aku tidak melupakannya)

ANSWER: Prepositional phrase as adverb.

'into my purse' tells where the shopping list needs to be put. 'ke dalam dompetku' menyatakan tempat di mana daftar belanja harus diletakkan. Maka, prepositional phrase ini berfungsi sebagai kata keterangan (adverb).

d. We will order pizza during the break.

(Kami akan memesan pizza selama jam istirahat)

ANSWER: Prepositional phrase as adverb.

'during the break' tells when we will order the pizza. 'selama jam istirahat' menyatakan waktu kami akan memesan pizza. Maka, prepositional phrase ini berfungsi sebagai kata keterangan (adverb).

PembahasanPrepositional Phrases

Prepositional Phrases adalah frasa yang menggunakan preposisi. Frasa ini dapat berfungsi sebagai kata sifat (adjectives) dan sebagai kata keterangan (adverbs).

Prepositional Phrase as adjectives memodifikasi kata benda atau kata ganti yang menjelaskan 'which', 'what kind', 'how much/many'. Prepositional Phrase as adjectives mengikuti kata benda atau kata ganti.

Contoh:

The computer in that room is broken. (which)Is the book on the table yours? (which)People at railway station are waiting for the train. (which)The tree in front of my house is dying. (which)

Prepositional Phrase as adverbs memodifikasi kata kerja, kata sifat, atau kata keterangan yang menjelaskan 'where', 'when', 'how', or 'why'. Prepositional Phrase as adverbs dapat diletakkan di mana saja tanpa mengubah maknanya.

Contoh:

I will go to bookstore on Sunday. (when)I put all your belonging in your room. (where)I was at home when the accident happened. (where)The event was cancelled because of heavy rain. (why)

30. makes 5 prepositional phrase?​


Jawaban:

A prepositional phrase is a group of words that lacks either a verb or a subject, and that functions as a unified part of speech. It normally consists of a preposition and a nounor a preposition and a pronoun.

Remember the following rules for prepositional phrases and you will find that using them becomes much easier.

Prepositional phrases always consist of two basic parts at minimum: the object and the preposition.

In formal English, prepositions are almost always followed by objects.

Adjectives can be placed between the prepositions and objects in prepositional phrases.

Prepositional phrases can act as adverbs or adjectives. When they are used as adjectives, they modify nouns and pronouns in the same way single-word adjectives do.

When prepositional phrases are used as adverbs, they at the same way single-word adverbs and adverb clauses do, modifying adjectives, verbs, and other adverbs.

Just like bread and cheese are the minimum ingredients for making a cheese sandwich, a preposition and the object of a preposition are the minimum requirements for forming a prepositional phrase. The object of a preposition is the name for the noun or pronoun following the preposition.

Prepositional phrases don’t have to be plain. Just as adding more ingredients to a sandwich dresses it up, adding more words such as adverbs or adjectives is a good method for spicing up prepositional phrases. As you read the following examples of prepositional phrases, you’ll notice that the first sentence in each set contains a basic prepositional phrase, while the second one contains a more interesting one.
